Privacy Policy

Last Updated: November 9, 2025

GigMate ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our platform.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide

  • Account Information: Name, email, phone number, password
  • Profile Information: Bio, photos, stage name, venue details, social media links
  • Payment Information: Credit card details (processed by Stripe, not stored by us)
  • Booking Information: Event details, agreements, contracts
  • Communication: Messages, reviews, ratings, support inquiries

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Device Information: IP address, browser type, device type, operating system
  • Usage Data: Pages viewed, features used, time spent, clicks, searches
  • Location Data: GPS coordinates (with permission), city, state, zip code
  • Cookies: Session cookies, preference cookies, analytics cookies

1.3 Information from Third Parties

  • Social Media: Profile information if you link social accounts
  • Payment Processors: Transaction confirmations from Stripe
  • Analytics Providers: Aggregated usage statistics

2. How We Use Your Information

Purpose Information Used

3. How We Share Your Information

3.1 With Other Users

Your public profile information is visible to other users, including:

  • Name, photo, bio, ratings, reviews
  • Performance history, availability
  • Social media links (if provided)

3.2 With Service Providers

  • Stripe: Payment processing
  • Supabase: Data storage and authentication
  • Google: Maps and location services
  • Resend: Email delivery
  • Analytics providers: Usage tracking (anonymized)

3.3 For Business Purposes

  • Investors: Aggregated, anonymized analytics
  • Advertisers: Demographic data (no personal info)
  • Data buyers: Market insights (anonymized)

3.4 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information when required by law or to:

  • Comply with legal process
  • Protect rights, property, and safety
  • Prevent fraud or security threats
  • Enforce our Terms of Service

4. Your Privacy Rights

4.1 Access and Control

You have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of your data
  • Correct: Update inaccurate information
  • Delete: Request account deletion
  • Export: Download your data
  • Opt-out: Unsubscribe from marketing emails

4.2 California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

California residents have additional rights:

  • Know what personal information is collected
  • Know if personal information is sold or disclosed
  • Opt-out of the sale of personal information
  • Request deletion of personal information
  • Non-discrimination for exercising rights

4.3 European Privacy Rights (GDPR)

EU residents have rights under GDPR:

  • Right to access personal data
  • Right to rectification
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object to processing

5. Data Retention

We retain your information for as long as:

  • Your account is active
  • Needed to provide services
  • Required by law or regulations
  • Necessary for dispute resolution

After account deletion: We retain transaction records for 7 years (legal requirement) but delete profile and personal information within 30 days.

6. Data Security

We implement security measures including:

  • Encryption: TLS 1.3 for data in transit, AES-256 for data at rest
  • Access Controls: Role-based access, multi-factor authentication
  • Monitoring: 24/7 security monitoring and alerts
  • Regular Audits: Security assessments and penetration testing
  • Secure Infrastructure: Enterprise-grade cloud providers

Note: No method of transmission over the Internet is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Cookies and Tracking

7.1 Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: Required for platform functionality
  • Analytics Cookies: Understand how users interact with the platform
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your settings and choices
  • Marketing Cookies: Deliver relevant advertisements

7.2 Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through:

  • Browser settings
  • Our cookie preference center
  • Third-party opt-out tools

See our Cookie Policy for more details.

8. Children's Privacy

GigMate is not intended for users under 18. We do not knowingly collect information from children. If we learn we have collected information from a child under 18, we will delete it immediately.

9. International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your own. We ensure appropriate safeguards through:

  • Standard contractual clauses
  • Privacy Shield frameworks (where applicable)
  • Service providers' compliance certifications

10. Third-Party Links

Our platform may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these sites. We encourage you to read their privacy policies.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. We will notify you of material changes via:

  • Email notification
  • Platform announcement
  • Updated "Last Updated" date

Continued use after changes constitutes acceptance.
