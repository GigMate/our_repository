This Cookie Policy explains how GigMate uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our platform. By using GigMate, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences and improve your experience.

Types of Storage Technologies

  • Cookies: Small text files stored by your browser
  • Local Storage: Browser storage for larger amounts of data
  • Session Storage: Temporary storage cleared when browser closes
  • IndexedDB: Database storage for complex data

5. Managing Cookie Preferences

5.1 Cookie Settings

You can control cookies through our cookie preference center:

  • Essential cookies cannot be disabled
  • Toggle analytics cookies on/off
  • Toggle preference cookies on/off
  • Toggle marketing cookies on/off

5.2 Browser Settings

Chrome:

  1. Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies
  2. Choose: Allow all, Block third-party, or Block all
  3. Manage exceptions for specific sites

Firefox:

  1. Settings → Privacy & Security
  2. Choose: Standard, Strict, or Custom
  3. Manage cookies and site data

Safari:

  1. Preferences → Privacy
  2. Choose to block all cookies or allow from current website
  3. Manage website data

Edge:

  1. Settings → Cookies and site permissions
  2. Choose: Allow all, Block third-party, or Block all
  3. Manage and delete cookies

5.3 Mobile Devices

iOS Safari:

  1. Settings → Safari → Privacy & Security
  2. Block All Cookies or Prevent Cross-Site Tracking

Android Chrome:

  1. Chrome → Settings → Site settings → Cookies
  2. Choose cookie preferences

6. Impact of Disabling Cookies

Blocking certain cookies may affect your experience:

Cookie Type If Disabled
Essential Cannot use the platform (login, payments won't work)
Analytics No impact on functionality, but we can't improve the platform as effectively
Preference Settings not saved, need to re-enter preferences each visit
Marketing You'll still see ads, but they may be less relevant

7. Do Not Track (DNT)

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" feature. Currently, there is no industry standard for responding to DNT signals. We do not respond to DNT signals but offer cookie controls as described above.
